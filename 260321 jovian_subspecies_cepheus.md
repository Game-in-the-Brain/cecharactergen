**Jovians**  
Homo sapiens jovianus — "Spinners," "Beltwalkers," "Rad-boys"  
Jovians are the workers and residents of the outer tether habitats — the enormous rotating cables that orbit Jupiter, harvesting atmospheric materials and servicing the skimming operations that feed the entire Jovian States economy. They live in the high-G zones of those tethers, 1,000–3,000 km from the rotation axis, where centripetal acceleration runs between 1.2G and 1.8G. Their bones are dense, their frames are stocky, and their skin carries the faint grey-brown mottling of the Radosymbiont — the fungal colony that has become as much a part of a Jovian body as the liver or the lung.  
Jovians emerged as a significant population of 1M, in near the end 23rd century, they began as the outer spin habitat service workers in the early 23rd century when Nano-tube productions scaled up. They didnt have the high-G body disymorphia that allowed people to know them from afar until the end of the 23rd century. Early on what identified them was the Radosymbiot and the mottling. They were mostly Vat grown, until early 24th centtury when mothers had a treatment that allowed their radosymbiot protect the embryo by a brilliant reproductive health Doctor named Gitya.   
By the 24th Century, their population exploded because of the incredible demand for as many possible Skimming passes can be scheduled and larger and larger Tethers allowed for deeper and deeper gouging passes with Jovian forges.

 **1. JOVIANS**  
   
 *Homo sapiens jovianus — "Spinners," "Beltwalkers," "Rad-boys"*  
   
 Jovians are the workers and residents of the outer tether habitats — the enormous rotating cables that orbit Jupiter, harvesting atmospheric materials and servicing the skimming operations that feed the entire Jovian States economy. They live in the high-G zones of those tethers, 1,000–3,000 km from the rotation axis, where centripetal acceleration runs between 1.2G and 1.8G. Their bones are dense, their frames are stocky, and their skin carries the faint grey-brown mottling of the Radosymbiont — the fungal colony that has become as much a part of a Jovian body as the liver or the lung.  
   
 **Appearance**  
   
 Jovians stand 15–20% shorter than the Terrestrial average for their sex, with proportionally wider shoulders, thicker limb bones, and a heavier brow ridge. They are often described as looking "pressed down" — which is accurate. The Radosymbiont expresses as a subtle mottling across sun-exposed skin areas, most visible on the forearms, neck, and face. In some family lines it forms distinct grey-brown patches; in others it is nearly invisible. Eyes tend toward lighter irises, possibly a Radosymbiont-adjacent adaptation to the radiation environment. Jovians in environments below 1.5G develop visible puffiness in the face and extremities within hours — the cardiovascular system over-pumping against a gravity gradient its owners were never designed for.  
   
 **Biology**  
   
 **The Radosymbiont** (*Trichofungus radiovora*, informal designation) is a colony organism distributed across the skin, with denser populations in specialized subdermal organs — colloquially called "bloom organs" or "bread organs" — concentrated in the upper back and shoulders. These organs cultivate and feed the surface colony, maintaining its population density. The Radosymbiont metabolizes ionizing radiation, breaking down high-energy particles into metabolic byproducts. It does not make Jovians immune to radiation — it raises the threshold significantly and provides continuous low-level protection. At sufficient radiation flux the symbiont is actively   *fed*, and a well-fed Radosymbiont provides measurable protection against cumulative dose.  
   
 A Jovian deprived of background radiation for extended periods will experience **Symbiont Hunger** — the Radosymbiont begins metabolizing host tissue to survive, causing fatigue, skin lesions, and eventually immune dysregulation. This is rarely fatal if caught early but is chronically unpleasant. Jovians working in shielded environments for extended periods carry small   **bloom packs** — personal devices emitting low-level gamma at safe-but-nutritive flux — which they wear against the skin under their suits.  
   
 Jovians are mildly radioactive. The Radosymbiont re-emits a fraction of absorbed radiation as low-energy secondary emission. This is comparable to background space radiation and poses no acute hazard in brief contact. However, prolonged close-quarters cohabitation with non-Jovians — sleeping in the same space, working in close proximity for months — produces a cumulative dose that non-adapted humans would rather avoid. **Standard vacc suits worn by Jovians include a 2mm lead-foil lining on the exterior** to attenuate secondary emission during extended contact operations. This is social convention as much as safety requirement; most Jovians are scrupulous about suit discipline around Terrestrials.  
   
 **Cepheus Engine Statistics**  
   
 **Characteristic Modifiers**  
- **STR** +1 (dense bone, high-G musculature)  
- **END** +1 (Radosymbiont metabolic support, high-G cardiovascular conditioning)  
- **DEX** –2 in gravity below 1.5G (bloating, cardiovascular over-pressure, proprioceptive mismatch)  
 **Generation Rule — High-G Advantage**  
   
    
   
  When generating STR and END, roll **3d6 and keep the highest 2** (instead of the standard 2d6). Apply the +1 modifier after rolling.  
 **Special Traits**  
 *Radosymbiont Protection*  
   
    
   
  Jovians have natural **Radiation Resistance 2**. Reduce all radiation exposure rolls by 2 before applying effects. At high radiation flux (Jupiter proximity, active belt operations), increase this to Radiation Resistance 4 — the symbiont is actively feeding and performing at peak.  
 *Symbiont Hunger*  
   
    
   
  If a Jovian spends more than 2 weeks in a radiation-shielded environment without a bloom pack, they begin experiencing Symbiont Hunger. Apply –1 END per week until bloom pack use resumes or exposure to background radiation occurs. Recovery is 1 END per day of adequate exposure. A Jovian reduced to END 0 by Symbiont Hunger is critically ill.  
 *Low-G Intolerance*  
   
    
   
  In gravity below 1.5G: DEX –2 (cumulative with characteristic modifier, minimum DEX 1).  
   
    
   
  In gravity below 0.5G: DEX –3, STR –2 (vascular pooling, reduced coordination).  
   
    
   
  In microgravity / zero-G: Jovians require the **Zero-G skill** at level 0 or better to function without penalty. Untrained Jovians in zero-G are at –2 to all physical tasks and suffer nausea penalties at the GM's discretion.  
 *High-G Adaptation*  
   
    
   
  Jovians suffer no penalties in gravity up to 2G. Between 2G and 3G, apply standard high-G fatigue rules. Above 3G, standard rules apply.  
 *Pressure Adaptation*  
   
    
   
  Jovians are acclimated to 2 ATM. In standard 1 ATM environments they experience mild sinus discomfort and reduced olfactory acuity (no mechanical effect). In environments below 0.8 ATM they suffer –1 to all checks from dysbarism symptoms. In environments above 3 ATM, they gain +1 END for purposes of pressure-tolerance checks.  
 *Secondary Emission*  
   
    
   
  Non-Jovians in sustained close proximity to an unsuited Jovian (8+ hours per day, more than 2 consecutive weeks) accumulate radiation at approximately 2 rads/week. Jovians are expected to wear their emission-lined suits in mixed company. Failure to do so is a social offense in most Jovian States habitats, roughly equivalent to smoking indoors.  
 **Skills**  
   
    
   
  Jovians receive the following starting skills at character creation (in addition to background skills):  
- **Zero-G** 0 (basic awareness, even if untrained, due to cultural familiarity)  
- **Vacc Suit** 1 (suit discipline is instilled from childhood; Jovians spend their lives in them)  
 **Careers**  
   
    
   
  Jovians have unrestricted access to the following careers: Belter, Spacer, Engineer, Marine (Jovian States), Merchant. They may not enter the **Low-G Specialist** career without GM approval and a stated exceptional background.

 **2. LOW-G HUMANS (VOIDBORN)**  
 *Homo sapiens vacuensis — "Greens," "Chloros," "Vine-people," "Sap"*  
   
 Voidborn are the other major adaptive branch of Jovian States humanity — the long-duration spacers, habitat engineers, and tether hub workers who live and work in the microgravity and low-G zones near the rotation axes. They are leaner, taller, and slower-moving than Terrestrials, with the most immediately visible marker of their adaptation: the **Chlorosymbiont**, a colony of engineered cyanobacterial organisms distributed through their circulatory system and concentrated in subdermal vascular networks that show clearly through pale skin. A Voidborn in good health looks faintly green. Their veins, particularly on the forearms, neck, and face, are visible as bright blue-green traceries.  
 **Biology**  
   
 The **Chlorosymbiont** (*Cyanobacteria vaculis*, informal designation) performs photosynthesis using the host's circulatory CO₂ as a carbon source, producing glucose and oxygen and releasing them directly into the bloodstream. This provides meaningful metabolic supplementation — a Voidborn can extend endurance, reduce food requirements, and operate for longer periods on minimal life support. The Chlorosymbiont also provides a secondary radiation management function similar to the Jovian Radosymbiont, though less specialized: the organisms incorporate radiation into their metabolic cycle at low flux levels.  
   
 The Chlorosymbiont requires full-spectrum light — specifically strong in the red (660nm) and blue (450nm) bands — to photosynthesize effectively. Voidborn suits are equipped with **LED bloom panels** on the forearm and upper chest, and Voidborn habitats maintain high-output lighting in the relevant spectra. A Voidborn deprived of adequate light experiences   **Chloro-fade** — the symbiont population crashes, the green tinge fades, and the metabolic benefit is lost within days. Full recovery requires a week of adequate light exposure.  
 **Cepheus Engine Statistics**  
 **Characteristic Modifiers**  
- **AGI/DEX** +1 (zero-G adapted proprioception, lightweight frame)  
- **END** –1 (reduced mass, lower baseline metabolic reserve) *Note: offset by Chlorosymbiont when active*  
- **STR** –1 in gravity above 0.7G (cardiovascular strain, reduced bone density)  
 **Generation Rule — Low-G Advantage**  
   
    
   
  When generating DEX, roll **3d6 and keep the highest 2**.  
 **Special Traits**  
 *Chlorosymbiont — Metabolic Supplement*  
   
    
   
  When the Voidborn has received adequate light (4+ hours of full-spectrum exposure in the past 24 hours), they gain:  
- Reduce food requirements by half  
- Reduce oxygen consumption by 25% (extend life support duration accordingly)  
- **END** effectively +1 for fatigue and endurance checks only (not for damage or other purposes)  
- Radiation Resistance 1  
 *Chloro-fade*  
   
    
   
  Without adequate light for 48+ hours, the Chlorosymbiont begins to die. The Voidborn loses all Chlorosymbiont benefits. After 1 week without light, apply –1 END permanently until the symbiont is repopulated (requires medical intervention and 2d6 weeks of treatment).  
 *High-G Intolerance*  
   
    
   
  In gravity above 0.7G: STR –1, END –1 (cumulative with characteristic modifier).  
   
    
   
  In gravity above 1.2G: STR –2, END –2. Voidborn require **High-G medication** (available at TL10+) or vacc suit with active pressure support to operate above 1.2G for more than a few hours.  
 *Zero-G Native*  
   
    
   
  Voidborn ignore penalties in zero-G and microgravity. They receive **Zero-G skill** at level 1 free at character creation. They may substitute DEX for STR in zero-G physical tasks.  
 *Visible Veins*  
   
    
   
  The Chlorosymbiont vascular network makes Voidborn immediately identifiable. Disguising this requires full skin coverage. Medical scanners will identify Chlorosymbiont presence. Some habitats and stations have entry restrictions on Voidborn due to symbiont contamination concerns (unfounded — the symbiont is non-transmissible without medical procedure).  
 **Skills**  
- **Zero-G** 1 (free at character creation)  
- **Vacc Suit** 0

 **3. CHLOROSYMBIONT TRANSPLANT (TERRESTRIAL PROCEDURE)**  
   
 Terrestrial humans and others may undergo **Chlorosymbiont transplantation** — a medical procedure available at TL11+ that introduces the engineered cyanobacterial colony into the recipient's circulatory system.  
 **The Procedure**  
   
 The transplant requires a course of immunosuppressants lasting 3–6 months while the symbiont establishes itself. The immune system never fully accepts the colony; maintenance doses of mild immunosuppressants are required for life. This permanent immune modulation has a lasting metabolic cost.  
 **Mechanical Effect of Transplant**  
- Permanent **END –1d3** (roll at time of procedure; this loss is not recoverable)  
- Recipient gains all Chlorosymbiont benefits (Metabolic Supplement, Radiation Resistance 1)  
- Recipient develops the characteristic green tinge and visible veining within 3–6 months  
- Recipient is now subject to Chloro-fade rules  
- High-G Intolerance is **not** acquired — the body architecture remains Terrestrial  
 **Social Note**  
   
    
   
  Transplanted Terrestrials occupy a contested social space. Voidborn often regard them with ambivalence — the symbiont is real, the benefits genuine, but the underlying physiology is Terrestrial. Some Voidborn use the term "Dye-jobs" or "Painted" for transplants, though this varies widely by habitat culture.

 **4. HABITAT CONTEXT — THE M.A.G.I.C.I.A.N.S.**  
 *(Modular Articulated Gravitational Industrial Colony — Inner And Near Space)*  
   
 The spin habitats of the Jovian States are stratified by gravity, and by social class that has become inseparable from it. Moving outward from the rotation axis:  
   
 | | | |  
   
 |-|-|-|  
   
 | **Zone** |  **Gravity** |  **Population** |  
   
 | Hub core | 0 – 0.1G | Station operations, docking, some Voidborn specialists |  
   
 | Inner ring | 0.1 – 0.5G | Voidborn residential, light industry |  
   
 | Mid ring | 0.5 – 1.0G | Mixed Terrestrial / mild Half-Jovian |  
   
 | Outer ring | 1.0 – 1.5G | Terrestrial residential, standard industry |

 This is a great piece of hard SF bioengineering. Let me think through the actual physiology before designing the implant, because the mechanism needs to be correct to be useful for your setting.  
 **The real problem in microgravity**  
   
 On Earth, gravity pulls blood downward continuously — the cardiovascular system evolved to *fight* this, pumping hard to get blood up to the brain. In zero-G, that fight disappears. Blood pools in the upper body and head. Astronauts get puffy faces, stuffed sinuses, and intracranial pressure builds because the venous drainage that normally relies on gravity-assisted return simply stops working efficiently. The brain sits in cerebrospinal fluid inside a rigid skull — if venous pressure rises and CSF can't drain fast enough, intracranial pressure (ICP) climbs. Long-term this damages vision (spaceflight-associated neuro-ocular syndrome, SANS, is a real and serious problem NASA is actively researching).  
   
 So the implant isn't actually *relieving* pressure by adding drainage — it's replacing the **gravitational venous return** that the body loses in zero-G. The jugular veins normally drain passively downhill. In zero-G they don't. Your implant is an   **active jugular assist pump**.  
 **The Voidborn genetic solution**  
   
 What gene editing would actually do here is co-opt smooth muscle control in the jugular and cerebral venous system — adding rhythmic peristaltic pumping to veins that are normally passive. Veins have smooth muscle but it's not under the same neural control as arteries. Voidborn have essentially turned their major cranial venous drainage into a second, low-pressure active circulatory loop. This is elegant and metabolically cheap — it doesn't require a strong pump, just a coordinated squeeze.  
   
 The problem in high gravity is exactly what you described: those same veins, now doing active work against a gravity gradient *they weren't tuned for*, can't generate enough pressure differential to drain the brain effectively going uphill. So in 1G+ environments, Voidborn get the opposite problem — venous pooling in the cranium because their modified drainage system is working against gravity rather than with it, and it's not strong enough. Lightheadedness, pressure headaches, vision disturbances.

**CJVAP — Cranial Jugular Venous Assist Pump**  
**Common names:** "The sleeve," "juggle," "headcount" (slang for the check-up required before installation)  
   
 **Form:** A titanium-polymer peristaltic sleeve, approximately 14cm long, fitted around the internal jugular vein in the neck. Installed via minimally invasive surgery at TL11+. A hair-thin pressure sensor wire runs up to a micro-transducer anchored at the skull base, reading intracranial pressure continuously. A subcutaneous MCU module (about the size of a large coin) sits lateral to the sleeve, housing the control logic and an inductive charging coil — recharged wirelessly through the skin, typically during sleep.  
   
 **What it does:** The sleeve contains six independent peristaltic compression segments. Each can squeeze the vein in sequence, creating a travelling wave that assists or resists venous flow — exactly like the smooth muscle peristalsis of your gut, but electrically controlled and millisecond-precise. The MCU reads ICP from the skull sensor and adjusts pump rate and direction in a continuous closed loop. The target ICP window is 10–15 mmHg — within normal healthy range, never zero.  
   
 **In zero-G:** The pump runs actively, squeezing blood down and out of the cranial venous sinuses toward the heart at roughly the rate gravity would provide at 0.5G. ICP stays controlled. The user's face doesn't puff. Vision stays clear. The pump isn't *eliminating* the fluid shift — it's compensating for roughly half of it, which is enough to prevent the long-term damage of untreated cephalad fluid shift. Users still feel mildly "full-headed" in prolonged zero-G but don't suffer SANS-equivalent eye damage.  
   
 **In 1G:** The pump drops to standby. Gravity handles drainage. The sleeve monitors passively, only activating if ICP drifts above threshold — useful during Valsalva manoeuvres, high-intensity exertion, or illness.  
   
 **In high-G (1.5G+):** The pump reverses mode — now it assists *inflow*, pushing against the gravitational pull that would otherwise drain the cranial venous sinuses too aggressively and drop cerebral perfusion pressure. This is the mode that keeps Terrestrials functional in Jovian work zones.  
   
 **Failure modes:** If the MCU loses power, the sleeve goes passive — it's a loose collar around the vein, not a clamp. No immediate danger, but ICP begins drifting within hours in zero-G. A flat charge is a medical inconvenience, not an emergency. If the sensor wire breaks, the pump runs on a pre-programmed gravity estimate derived from the accelerometer in the MCU — less precise, but adequate until repair.  
   
 **Voidborn comparison:** The Voidborn genetic solution does exactly the same job using hijacked smooth muscle tissue in the jugular and cerebral venous walls — peristaltic, low-pressure, continuous. It is metabolically cheaper than the implant and more reliable (no battery, no electronics). The failure mode in high-G is that those muscles, tuned for zero-G drainage assist, simply cannot generate enough pressure differential against a gravitational gradient working against them. The CJVAP implant in its high-G reversal mode is actually *stronger* than the Voidborn solution — which is why Terrestrials with the implant can tolerate Jovian work zones better than unmodified Voidborn can.  
   
 This creates an interesting social texture: Voidborn are the native zero-G specialists, but a Terrestrial with a CJVAP and a good high-G suit can operate in more gravity ranges than either pure type. The implant is the great equaliser — and some Voidborn find that uncomfortable.  
   
 **Cepheus Engine / Mneme mechanic:** The CJVAP removes the zero-G ICP penalty for Terrestrial characters. It does not grant Zero-G skill. Without it, Terrestrials operating in prolonged microgravity (2+ weeks) accumulate a -1 END penalty per month from cephalad fluid shift, recoverable on return to gravity. With it, this penalty does not apply. Cost: TL11, Cr 45,000, requires surgical downtime of 1 week.
